    Clustering and Segmentation approach for geo level medical zones to deliver specific insights and recommendations.

    Client: Novartis Japan Hematology

    Risk-based Approach in Benign/chronic hematology for Optimization of Workloads

      Business Objective

    Integrate different data sources like Novartis internal data, DDD, Takumi-San etc. to get complete mapping of Haematologists to Hospitals and Hospitals to SMZs. Cluster SMZs on the basis of the Haematologists to patient ratio and other features. Develop a dashboard with a geo plot depicting the different SMZs and their segmentation for targeting.

      Context

    Due to lack of dedicated HEM clinics and ageing population, the number of patients outweighs Haematologists. This results in delay of care for high risk patients. Free-access allows less severe patients to visit specialists. Current data on the hospitals, HCPs, patients etc. exists in silos and is not streamlined.

      Business Outcome

    Enable all hematology disease patients to receive optimal diagnosis and treatment by supporting hematologists to manage workload with task-sharing approach.

      Data Sources

    Demographic: Population, Age, Area, Population Density, Binned Age, Projected Population
    Medical: Patient Count, Inpatient, Outpatient, Doctors, Doctors/capita, Nursing Facilities, No of Clinics, General Doctors, Hematologists, Specialist Count, No of Specialists in each Therapy, etc
    Sales

      Approach
    • Integrate different data sources like Novartis internal data, DDD, Takumi-San etc. to get complete mapping of Haematologists to Hospitals and Hospitals to SMZs.
    • Segregate variables based on Supply and Demand (Available slots vs Sales). This is done to obtain ratio later.
    • Analyse the characteristics of SMZs and cluster on the basis of the Haematologists to patient ratio and other important latent features.
    • Calculate (feature engineer) slope of yearly variables (population2015-16-17-18) to capture increase/decrease in trends and use them as variables.
    • Iterate with different methodologies of clustering including K-Means, DBScan, OPTICS, BIRCH, Spectral Clustering, Agglomerative Clustering, and Gaussian Mixture Models among others.
    • Develop a python dash based dashboard with a geo plot depicting the different SMZs and color coded segments.
      Algorithms

    Algorithms: K-Means, DBScan, OPTICS, BIRCH, Spectral Clustering, Agglomerative, Gaussian Mixture Models
    Validation: Silhouette Score, Davies Bouldin Score, Calinski Harabasz Score

      Project Outcome

    Clustering and Implementation was done efficiently.Results were specific to target 9% of SMZ to affect 42% hematologists and helping 32% patients in Japan

    Pricing Engine

    Exploratory Data Analysis to a Front end Web Application. This project revolved around collating price data to provide the right product price insights.

    Client: Novartis Global Pricing (Oncology)

    View Project Details

    Global Product Pricing Strategy

      Business Objective

    To develop and distribute a unified web application for Global Pricing Strategy team for Novartis Oncology that helps users decide a competitive price for a new lauch drug by comparing Pricing Analogs across the Oncology market.

      Context

    Pricing a drug is the key factor during a product launch. The Global Pricing Strategy team wants to have an analytical approach to setting the right price of a to-be-launching drug backed by data and analytics. The right price will be a combination of characteristics from a set of already launched drug analogs.

      Business Outcome
    • How to price the newly launched product and look into their projected sales and price evolution?
    • What are the best suited analogues for the brand that could help give a directional on price ?
    • Which are the characteristics that drive pricing decisions in different disease areas?
      Data Sources

    IQVIA, PRICENTRIC, KANTAR, IPSOS, FDA, European Medicines Agency, ClinicalTrials.gov
    Therapy/Indication: Therapy Area, Anatomy, Brief & Detailed Indication, Line of Therapy, Order of Entry, Orphan Launch, Immunotherapy, Chemotherapy, Targeted therapy
    Molecule: Molecule Type, Mechanism of Action, Order of Entry
    Patient Characteristics: Onset Age, Prevalance, 5yr Survival, Disease Type
    Efficacy & Safety: Overall Survival (OS), Progression Free Survival (PFS), Overall Response Rate (ORR), CR, Quality of Life, Clinical Trial Type, No of Patients in Trial, Adverse Events (Grade3/4), % discontinued, % Severe Adverse Events
    Approval: Phase of Approval, Fast Track/Breakthrough/Conditional/Priority/Accelerated Approval
    Combination Drugs: Number of Combi Molecules, Combi Drug Name, Type of Combi Drug, Combi (Targeted therapy/chemo), Combi company, Cost
    Competitor: Brand, Duration of Treatment, Chemo/Biologic, Offlabel, Launch Price

      Approach
    • Derive Importance for key features that drive sales (Catboost Model)
    • Filter brands based on selected country and product type
    • Calculate similarity between the launch brand and existing brands in the database (Gower dissimilarity metric)
    • Identify top K analogs using K Nearest Neighbors and Gower dissimilarity
    • Determine the price bracket and show pricing evolution for the analogs
    • Show analogs in a tabular format allowing users to select particular analogs of choice
    • Show Price vs Sales Evolution similarity characteristics
    • Show SHAP value based contribution of each variable to arrive at the projected price
      Algorithms

    Regression Random Forest, Catboost for Feature Importance and Predicted Pricing
    KNN Model with Gower Dissimilarity
    SHAP values for contribution plot

      Project Outcome

    Web application was designed in Python Plotly Dash framework and deployed to individual system using Docker

    ROI Analysis

    Analysis of individual Digital and Non Personal Campaigns for Novartis USA Commercial Marketing and recommendation on Return on Investment.

    Client: USA Commercial Acceleration

    View Project Details

    Campaign Evaluation and Return on Investment Analysis

      Business Objective

    To evaluate digital and non-digital promotional campaigns and find out the Return on Investment

      Context

    The US Brand teams run digital promotions to HCPs and want to evaluate if a particular promotion was effective in generating sales and better reach patients.

      Business Outcome

    Generate awareness about a particular brand to specialist HCPs so that more patients are served. HCP List Created > Forwarded to Brand Team > List forwarded to Vendors > Target Test Group is created > Campaign Runs (3 mo) > Engagement List Forwarded back > Run Analysis > Compare Test with Control

      Data Sources

    Physican: TRx, NRx, NBRx Sales, Market Behaviour, Calls, Samples
    Activity: Emails / Digital Newsletters / Targeted Website Banner Ads / Professional Service Sites - Opened, Clicked Through

      Approach

    Starting from collating the data to delivering the recommendations in terms of ROI, the steps below list out the process:

    • Collate the data for the particular brand. Find the market universe.
    • Data is separated based on T and C and interaction variables are created. The original variables are also left in case the interaction variables turn out insignificant
    • Avg Post TRx is predicted based on PreTRx+PostMkt+PostCalls+PostSamp for both the Test and Control HCPs
    • The Pre Period variables are excluded as the T and C are matched basis that and would turn up correlated in the model.
    • Model is run and if the variables are insignificant/negative coefficients, the interaction variables are removed and the total variable is used
    • One equation is generated with both T and C variables residing in the model. The T and C variables are separated and separate equations are generated.
    • Equation_T (Y = β1.X1+.....) for Test HCPs is generated using the X as Test_Average for each of the Independent Variables
    • Equation_C (Y = β1.X1+.....) for Control HCPs is generated using the X as Test_Average for each of the Independent Variables
    • Use Equation_T to calculate the Post Period TRx for each Test HCP
    • Use Equation_C to calculate the Post Period TRx for each Test HCP
    • The difference between, Equation_T - Equation_C gives us the lift for each Test HCP, essentially saying that if the Test HCPs did not recieve the campaign vs if the Test HCPs recieved the campaign
    • Revenue = (Lift * #HCPs * #Months * $/TRx) || ROI = Revenue/Cost
      Algorithms

    Test Control Methodology (Strata/1:1 Match)
    T-test for validation
    ANCOVA based regression modeling

      Project Outcome

    Results were provided across a wide range of Campaigns starting from Email Marketing to App based notifications. Insights gererated were aligned with full flegded Marketing Mix Models.

    To know how each individual marketing channel performed in the last year.
